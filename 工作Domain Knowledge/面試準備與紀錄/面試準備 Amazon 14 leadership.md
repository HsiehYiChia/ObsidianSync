# 14 leadership

[https://kraftshala.com/what-questions-to-expect-in-amazon-interview/](https://kraftshala.com/what-questions-to-expect-in-amazon-interview/)

**1. Customer Obsession**

Leaders start with the customer and work backward. They work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust. Although leaders pay attention to competitors, they obsess over customers.

- Who was your most difficult customer?
- Give me an example of a time when you did not meet a client’s expectation. What happened, and how did you attempt to rectify the situation?
- When you’re working with a large number of customers, it’s tricky to deliver excellent service to them all. How do you go about prioritizing your customers’ needs?
- Tell the story of the last time you had to apologize to someone.

**2. Ownership**

Leaders are owners. They think long term and don’t sacrifice long-term value for short-term results. They act on behalf of the entire company, beyond just their own team. They never say “that’s not my job.

- Tell me about a time when you had to leave a task unfinished.
- Tell me about a time when you had to work on a project with unclear responsibilities.

**3. Invent and Simplify**

Leaders expect and require innovation and invention from their teams and always find ways to simplify. They are externally aware, look for new ideas from everywhere, and are not limited by “not invented here”. As we do new things, we accept that we may be misunderstood for long periods of time.

- Tell me about a time when you gave a simple solution to a complex problem.
- Tell me about a time when you invented something.

**4. Are Right, A Lot**

Leaders are right a lot. They have strong judgment and good instincts. They seek diverse perspectives and work to disconfirm their beliefs.

- Tell me about a time when you were wrong.
- Tell me about a time when you had to work with incomplete data or information.

**5. Learn and Be Curious**

Leaders are never done learning and always seek to improve themselves. They are curious about new possibilities and act to explore them.

- Tell me about a time when you influenced a change by only asking questions.
- Tell me about a time when you solved a problem through just superior knowledge or observation.

**6. Hire and Develop The Best**

Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and promotion. They recognize exceptional talent and will move them throughout the organization. Leaders develop leaders and take seriously their role in coaching others. We work on behalf of our people to invent mechanisms for development like Career Choice.

- Tell me about a time when you mentored someone.Tell me about a time when you made a wrong hire. When did you figure it out and what did you do?

**7. Insist on the Highest Standards**

Leaders have relentlessly high standards – many people may think these standards are unreasonably high. Leaders are continually raising the bar and driving their teams to deliver high-quality products, services, and processes. Leaders ensure that defects do not get sent down the line and that problems are fixed so they stay fixed.

- Tell me about a time when you couldn’t meet your own expectations on a project.
- Tell me about a time when a team member didn’t meet your expectations on a project.

**8. Think Big**

Thinking small is a self-fulfilling prophecy. Leaders create and communicate a bold direction that inspires results. They think differently and look around corners for ways to serve customers.

- Tell me about your proudest professional achievement.
- Tell me about a time when you went way beyond the scope of the project and delivered.

**9. Bias for Action**

Speed matters in business. Many decisions and actions are reversible and do not need extensive study. We value calculated risk taking.

- Describe a time when you saw some problem and took the initiative to correct it rather than waiting for someone else to do it.
- Tell me about a time when you took a calculated risk.
- Tell me about a time you needed to get information from someone who wasn’t very responsive. What did you do?

**10. Frugality**

Accomplish more with less. Constraints breed resourcefulness, self-sufficiency, and invention. There are no extra points for growing headcount, budget size or fixed expense.

- Tell me about a time when you had to work with limited time or resources.

**11. Earn Trust**

Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully. They are vocally self-critical, even when doing so is awkward or embarrassing. Leaders do not believe their or their team’s body odour smells of perfume. They benchmark themselves and their teams against the best.

- What would you do if you found out that your closest friend at work was stealing?
- Tell me about a time when you had to tell someone a harsh truth.

**12. Dive Deep**

Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to the details, audit frequently, and are skeptical when metrics and anecdote differ. No task is beneath them.

- Give me two examples of when you did more than what was required in any job experience.Tell me about something that you learnt recently in your role.

**13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit**

Leaders are obligated to respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree, even when doing so is uncomfortable or exhausting. Leaders have conviction and are tenacious. They do not compromise for the sake of social cohesion. Once a decision is determined, they commit wholly.

- Tell me about a time when you did not accept the status quo.
- Tell me about an unpopular decision of yours.
- Tell me about a time when you had to step up and disagree with a team members approach.
- If your direct manager was instructing you to do something you disagreed with, how would you handle it?

**14. Deliver Results**

Leaders focus on the key inputs for their business and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion. Despite setbacks, they rise to the occasion and never settle.

- By providing an example, tell me when you have had to handle a variety of assignments. Describe the results.
- What is the most difficult situation you have ever faced in your life? How did you handle it?
- Give me an example of a time when you were 75% of the way through a project, and you had to pivot strategy–how were you able to make that into a success story?

(Build SOP)

We used to have proprietary legacy SDK, but our EasyMesh daemons were developed on the new OpenWrt SDK. At that time most of our customers use legacy SDK. Therefore, I have to port the daemons back to legacy SDK and write build SOP for them. After some work, I finish the porting and begin with the build SOP. In the build SOP I write down all the changes I made in it. However, something was missed in that build SOP and didn't notice that. I didn't follow my own build SOP and start from scratch but just write down what I've remembered. So when our customers try to port with the SOP, they failed. I apologize to them and I promise this will never happen again. I told them I will always check twice, and will always try it myself to make sure the SOP has no mistakes. I learn that we should be careful and always take it seriously when it comes to releasing to customers. Customer relation is very important

(use VIM)

In the beginning, I used source insight as my editor. We use Linux server in our daily work. We fetch and compile code on the server. - Hardest problem you solve?

(`dive deep`, `earn trust`, `frugality`, `deliver result`)

(AMSDU)

There is a WDS table that maintains WDS client in our driver. Onetime, the customer report that the WDS table is messed up because the AP receives corrupted packets. We have to fix it ASAP or the customer cannot continue their work. The first thing we do is to check whether it a tx problem or rx problem. From sniffer, the packet is all good. So we think the problem is on RX side. We tcpdump from Rx AP and notice that the packet is somehow incorrect. There should have something wrong in Rx AP. We then add debug code to dump information of the abnormal packet(RXD → RXD + payload). From the RXD it shows the abnormal is all good. The H/W indicators show that there has no error decrypting the packet, but the packet still corrupted. To get more information, we added log to dump the whole packet including RXD, header, payload and discover there are extra 2 bytes after RXD in the abnormal packet. We discuss it with our H/W design team and they said it because of padding. Our driver didn't not parse the 4 addr H/W DeAMSDU packet correct. The solution is to shift 2 bytes for data pointer. We also feedback the solution to our internal to prevent error for others.The customer is satisfied that we solve the issue with limited information. The solution is trivial but it's not that easy to find what was wrong in the system. We have to carefully check every stage to find out where is the problem.

(wifi release framework)

After we firstly migrate to OpenWrt, the release workflow involved many manual works. Our source codes are consist of several git repos, however, what we release to the customer are tarballs. We have to collect packages one by one, modify the makefile, rename the file, and then build the SDK to make sure it's ok. It requires a lot of time and has a big chance to make mistakes. My boss was impressed by the work I've done, and I earn trust from him. After that, my script is used for all of the wifi releasing task at Mediatek.

(Tx 6Mbps BW80 AP vs BW40 RPT)

Customer report a issue that there are 2 AP connected together, RootAP and Repeater AP, with bandwidth 80 and 40. When a client connects to RootAP, rate between RootAP and Repeater will drop to 6Mbps. What we only know was the scenario. Log and sniffer did not provide useful information. With limited information, we can just guess and make experiments to verify our assumption. We make several assumptions and then verify by experiments, but they all not related to the issue. With all other factor has been excluded, it leads to that there is something wrong with our RA algorithm. We discuss with our RA owner about the situation, we add debug logs to FW, and found out there is an auto bandwidth problem in the module. Finally, we deliver the patch to our customers, and they are satisfied by this because with this limited information, we still dig into the issue and came out with a solution.

(develop PREK)

My proudest professional achievement is I develop a feature called PREK. When our 1st wifi6 chip was launched, some of the features are not available. One of our customer from Japan they need this feature PREK for JAP radio regulation. I was assigned to get the job done. I make a plan to break down the feature into 7 smaller parts and finished them one by one. The feature was used when manufacturinging so there must be no potential crash issue in it. To achieve this, I wrote a test script that covers several common scenarios such as reentry, invalid arguments, invalid command sequence. I also have to collaborate with different people such as RF engineers and SA engineers in the task. Eventually, I finish the feature on time. The feature is widely used because it not only helpful for JAP radio regulation but also improves power on time and channel switch time.

(develop VLAN GTK)

One of our customer they want to separate BSS by VLAN, and they want each VLAN in each BSS can have its own GTK. I was responsible for the task. I start with surveying dynamic VLAN feature in RADIUS and hostapd. I asked clarifying questions the customer to make sure my full understanding of the feature, and I trace the code in hostapd and Linux kernel to know how vlans are handled. With these knowledge, I designed and developed the feature in our driver. There are 3 main parts: Register vlan netdev in kernel, Store vlan group key in H/W, Change group key when tx. I finish the feature step by step, and deliver the feature on time. With this feature, we can improve network security because the BCAST packet for a specific VLAN can only be decrypted by STA in that VLAN.
